1. Methodology
Two datasets were used: one containing market sentiment (Fear and Greed Index) and another with detailed trader activity.
After cleaning and converting timestamps to daily format, both datasets were merged by date.

2. Key metrics created included:
a. Daily profit and loss (PnL)
b. Trade frequency
c. Win rate
d. Trade size
e. Trade direction

Exploratory data analysis and visualizations were used to compare performance across different sentiment conditions.
Traders were also segmented based on risk level, trading frequency, and consistency.

3. Key Insights

A. Fear periods showed the highest trading activity and profitability

B. Greed periods had lower win rates and weaker performance

C. Traders placed larger trades during Extreme Greed

D. Infrequent traders often earned higher profits than frequent traders

E. High-risk traders performed well in Fear and Greed but poorly in Extreme Greed

Overall, market sentiment strongly influenced both trading behavior and performance.

4. Strategy Recommendations

a. Reduce risk and position size during Extreme Greed periods

b. Focus on fewer high-quality trades instead of overtrading

c. Use Fear periods as high-opportunity phases with strong risk management

Adapting strategies based on market mood can improve returns and reduce losses.

5. Limitations

A. Limited overlapping dates between sentiment and trading data

B. Other market factors such as volatility and price trends were not included

6. Future Scope

A. Use longer time periods for stronger analysis

B. Include technical indicators and market signals

C. Build predictive models to forecast trader performance
